=== What this evidence captures ===
The 7 contract assertions in the two negative-path describes
  "@BatchScheduled — invalid cron expression is rejected"
  "@BatchScheduled — invalid timezone is rejected"
FAIL against the RED stub. This is the RED half of the contract: it
locks in that the GREEN implementation MUST (a) reject ill-formed
cron expressions (empty / wrong field count / non-token literals)
and (b) reject invalid IANA timezones (unknown / empty / whitespace)
without silently registering.

The 5 vacuous / pre-wired assertions in the same negative-path filter
also pass against the stub and prove the implementation does not
OVER-reject: the 1 "well-known IANA zones" assertion (UTC / Asia/Seoul
/ America/New_York) is a positive counter-example inside the
"invalid timezone" describe, and the 4 inert-mode assertions pass
because the stub reads `process.env.BATCH_SCHEDULED_DISABLE` at
decoration time and stamps `inert: true|false` on the metadata.

  1 vacuous pass: well-known IANA zones — stub does not throw, so
                  `not.toThrow()` holds.
  4 inert passes:  stub stamps `inert` from the env var verbatim.

The 4 inert-mode assertions are intentionally NOT in the "validation
tests" category — they test pure metadata capture, not input
rejection. The follow-up GREEN task is the cron/timezone validation
half; inert capture is already in place here.
